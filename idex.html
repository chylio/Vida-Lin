Exploring Determinants of Holistic Care Competence:
Insights from Learner Roles and Professional Categories in Medical Education

Wen-Hsiu Lin, Shi-Yun Chen, Jui Sui Kao, Shu-Ya Lin, Chia-Te Liao

Medical Education Center, Chi Mei Medical Center, Tainan, Taiwan

Background

Holistic care, addressing physical, psychological, social, and spiritual dimensions, is a cornerstone of patient-centered care. Despite its importance, factors influencing holistic care competence across professional roles are insufficiently understood. This study explores the impact of professional roles, career stages, and interdisciplinary interactions on holistic care performance, providing guidance for curriculum design.

Summary of Work

Multidisciplinary case discussions were analyzed to assess holistic care competence across diverse professional roles (e.g., physicians, pharmacists, nurses). Metrics included physiological care quality, psychological and spiritual support, interpersonal challenge resolution, and integration of holistic care principles. Statistical methods, including ANOVA and post-hoc t-tests, identified role-specific differences. Subgroup analyses compared junior and senior physicians with allied healthcare professionals, and qualitative feedback enriched the quantitative findings.

Summary of Results

Significant differences emerged between roles. The chart below illustrates the average scores of various professional roles in specific care domains (higher numbers indicate better performance).

  • Allied Healthcare Professionals: Consistently excelled in physiological care (mean: 7.96, SD: 1.86) and psychological support (mean: 7.95, SD: 1.75).
  • Junior Physicians: Showed variability in interpersonal care (mean: 7.35, SD: 2.16).
  • Senior Physicians: Displayed strengths in psychological and spiritual support (mean: 8.20, SD: 1.50).

Discussion and Conclusion

Professional roles, career stages, and interdisciplinary exposure significantly shape holistic care competence. Allied healthcare professionals' consistent performance highlights their potential as key contributors to training. Targeted interventions, such as mentorship and experiential learning, are needed to address variability among physicians. Leveraging strengths of specific roles and fostering collaboration can enhance holistic care education, ensuring patient-centered care delivery.

Take-Home Messages

  • Holistic care competence is influenced by professional identity and interdisciplinary exposure.
